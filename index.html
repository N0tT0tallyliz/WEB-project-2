Treaty 1 - History and Impact



Education

Overview

Treaty 1 was signed on August 3, 1871, at Lower Fort Garry, Manitoba. It was the first of the Numbered Treaties between the Canadian government and Indigenous nations, mainly the Anishinaabe (Ojibway) and Swampy Cree.

Where i found the information for all this:Canadian Encylopedia

Negotiation Location

The treaty was negotiated at Lower Fort Garry, a historic Hudson's Bay Company trading post north of present-day Winnipeg.

First Nations Involved

  • Brokenhead Ojibway Nation
  • Fort Alexander (Sagkeeng First Nation)
  • Long Plain First Nation
  • Peguis First Nation
  • Roseau River Anishinaabe First Nation
  • Sandy Bay First Nation
  • Swan Lake First Nation

Key Terms of the Treaty

  • Each family of five was to receive 160 acres of land.
  • An annual annuity of $3 per person.
  • Provisions for farming tools, education, and clothing.
  • The government was to respect Indigenous hunting and fishing rights.

Modern Implementation

Treaty 1 still affects Indigenous communities today, with initiatives such as:

  • Urban Reserves: Naawi-Oodena (formerly Kapyong Barracks) in Winnipeg.
  • Economic Development: Long Plain First Nation's gas stations and businesses in Winnipeg.
