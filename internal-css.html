Treaties 1 to 5 (1871-1875)

These treaties were signed between the Canadian government and Indigenous Nations to share land and resources, but many promises were broken.

Learn More about the History of Treaties

Treaty 1 (1871) – Southern Manitoba (Winnipeg). Promised land, money, tools, and schools. Land disputes followed.
Treaty 2 (1871) – Southwestern Manitoba. Similar to Treaty 1 but with land concerns.
Treaty 3 (1873) – Northwestern Ontario & Manitoba. Promised land, hunting rights, and payments. Many misunderstandings.
Treaty 4 (1874) – Southern Saskatchewan & parts of Alberta/Manitoba. Promised land, payments, and education. Delays in support.
Treaty 5 (1875) – Central/Northern Manitoba & Ontario. Promised land, hunting rights, and schools. Hard to enforce due to distance.

These treaties still impact Indigenous rights in Canada today.
